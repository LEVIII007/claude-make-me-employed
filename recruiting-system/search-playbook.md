# Search Playbook

Use this as the default search approach unless Shashank says otherwise.

## LinkedIn

Search ideas:

- "software engineer" "0-1 years"
- "software engineer" "new grad"
- "backend engineer" "0-1 years"
- "backend engineer" "fresher"
- "software engineer intern"

Company-tier search ideas:

- Enterprise / MNC: add terms like "graduate", "associate", "campus", "early career", or specific company names
- Large Public Company: search direct company pages plus "new grad", "software engineer I", and "associate software engineer"
- Late-Stage Startup / Unicorn: search company names with "backend engineer", "software engineer", "platform engineer", and "India"
- Growth-Stage Startup / Scale-Up: search founder posts, engineering lead posts, and direct hiring pages with "SDE I", "backend engineer", or "software engineer"

What to filter for:

- Fresh postings first
- Entry-level or associate roles
- Titles that are broad enough to fit strong internship experience
- Company pages when Easy Apply results are weak
- Tag each promising lead with a company tier before deciding whether to apply
- Treat LinkedIn posts with multiple outbound links as ambiguous by default. Do not assume the first short link is the real apply link.
- When a post contains multiple outbound links, either save every plausible apply link or verify which one is the true company/career link before saving the lead.
- Roundup posts often mix real apply links with Telegram, WhatsApp, referral, promo, or channel links. Capture that distinction explicitly.

## X

Search ideas:

- "hiring software engineer"
- "hiring backend engineer"
- "new grad software engineer"
- "fresher hiring software engineer"
- "software engineer internship"
- combine with company names, founder names, or cities when useful

Company-tier search ideas:

- Enterprise / MNC: search for off-campus or early-career announcement posts
- Large Public Company: search engineering team hiring posts and recruiter threads
- Late-Stage Startup / Unicorn: search founders, CTOs, and talent partners by company name
- Growth-Stage Startup / Scale-Up: search for direct founder hiring posts and smaller engineering team threads

What to look for:

- Founder or engineering-lead posts
- Hiring threads
- Replies with application links
- Signals that the role is still open

## Triage rule

Prefer roles that satisfy most of these:

- 0-1 years or clearly fresher-friendly
- Backend or general software engineering
- Good fit with internships and projects
- Recent posting
- Clear application path
- Compensation seems reasonable
- Company tier is one of the four target buckets

## Tier-specific triage lens

- Enterprise / MNC: strong if the role is official early-career hiring with direct application access
- Large Public Company: strong if the role is backend or platform aligned and not quietly mid-level
- Late-Stage Startup / Unicorn: strong if the team is established enough to onboard a fresher-level engineer well
- Growth-Stage Startup / Scale-Up: strong if the JD values shipping, internships, and project depth more than years alone

## Do not rush into

- Old posts with no active link
- Roles asking for clearly senior ownership
- Internships below the compensation threshold
- Startups where the role is really a founding engineer job in disguise

## When search turns into apply

- Prefer direct company forms over social posts or aggregator pages.
- Start with companies where the user is already signed in or where no new account is needed.
- Verify that resume upload works before opening multiple applications.
- Keep a lightweight handoff list of tabs that are blocked by login, uploads, or new questions so they can be resumed instead of restarted.
